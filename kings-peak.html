Kings Peak

Kings Peak, located in the Uinta Mountains, is the tallest mountain in Utah with an elevation of 13,528 feet (4,123 m). It is situated in Duchesne County and is part of the Ashley National Forest. It is known for being a remote and strenuous hike, usually requiring a 2-3 day backpacking trip to reach the summit. Mileage: 29 Miles Elevation Gain: 5500 Feet Time: 2-3 Days to Summit
