Borah Peak

Mount Borah (also known as Borah Peak) is the tallest mountain in Idaho, standing at an elevation of 12,662 to 12,668 feet. Located in the Lost River Range within the Salmon-Challis National Forest, it is a challenging, non-technical climb known for the "Chicken Out Ridge" and a steep 5,500-foot elevation gain. Mileage: 8.4 Miles Elevation Gain: 5,600 Feet Time: 8-12 Hours
